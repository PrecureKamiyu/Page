THE FALSE ALARM

Although having tended to be an opponent of the government early in life, Johnson published a series of pamphlets throughout the 1770s in favour of various government policies. In 1770 he produced The False Alarm, a political pamphlet attacking the radical politician John Wilkes. In 1771, his Thoughts on the Late Transactions Respecting Falkland’s Islands cautioned against war with Spain, while in 1774 the author printed The Patriot, a critique of what he viewed as false patriotism.
