Single-track Latency: The Rotational Delay

To understand how a request would be processed on our simple, one- track disk, imagine we now receive a request to read block 0. How should the disk service this request?

In our simple disk, the disk doesn’t have to do much. In particular, it must just wait for the desired sector to rotate under the disk head. This wait happens often enough in modern drives, and is an important enough component of I/O service time, that it has a special name: rotational de- lay (sometimes rotation delay, though that sounds weird).
