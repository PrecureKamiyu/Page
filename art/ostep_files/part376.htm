36.10 Summary

You should now have a very basic understanding of how an OS inter- acts with a device. Two techniques, the interrupt and DMA, have been introduced to help with device efficiency, and two approaches to access- ing device registers, explicit I/O instructions and memory-mapped I/O, have been described. Finally, the notion of a device driver has been pre- sented, showing how the OS itself can encapsulate low-level details and thus make it easier to build the rest of the OS in a device-neutral fashion.
