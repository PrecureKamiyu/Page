DATABASE DESIGN‌

The task of creating a database application is a complex one, involving design of the database schema, design of the programs that access and update the data, and design of a security scheme to control access to data. The needs of the users play a central role in the design process. In this part, we focus primarily on the design of the database schema. We also outline some of the other design tasks.

The entity-relationship (E-R) model described in Chapter 6 is a high-level data model. Instead of representing all data in tables, it distinguishes between basic objects, called entities, and relationships among these objects. It is often used as a ﬁrst step in database-schema design.

Relational database design— the design of the relational schema— was covered in- formally in earlier chapters. There are, however, principles that can be used to distin- guish good database designs from bad ones. These are formalized by means of several “normal forms” that oﬀer diﬀerent trade-oﬀs between the possibility of inconsistencies and the eﬃciency of certain queries. Chapter 7 describes the formal design of relational schemas.
