Solution to Problem 1.1 (page 22)

This problem illustrates that Amdahl's law applies to more than just computer systems.

  1. In terms of Equation 1.1, we have α = 0.6 and k = 1.5. More directly, traveling the 1,500 kilometers through Montana will require 10 hours, and the rest of the trip also requires 10 hours. This will give a speedup of 25/(10 + 10) = 1.25×.

  2. In terms of Equation 1.1, we have α = 0.6, and we require S = 1.67, from which we can solve for k. More directly, to speed up the trip by 1.67×, we must decrease the overall time to 15 hours. The parts outside of Montana will still require 10 hours, so we must drive through Montana in 5 hours. This requires traveling at 300 km/hr, which is pretty fast for a truck!
