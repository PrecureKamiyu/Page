Fun Fact (seriously)

Conjugate and Conjugation

In language, to conjugate means to inflect a verb in its forms for distinctions such as number, person, voice, mood, and tense1. For example, the verb “to be” can be conjugated to form the words “am”, “is”, “are”, “was”, “were”, etc.
